Global Mortality Insights

The causes of death and mortality rates are essential data worth exploring for the health status of each country's population. However, mortality has not received significant attention. Diseases not only lead to death but also cause people to suffer while they are alive.

The following data visualization will show data for 31 different causes of death in countries around the world. These data visualizations will highlight the causes and countries with the highest mortality rates. The aim is to draw attention to the world's growing health problems

Map Location

This map shows the total number of deaths in each country over 10 years. The diverging colors used in the map show and emphasize number of deaths in each country

We can see that China and India, which are located in Asia, have the highest number of deaths of all countries in the world.

In North America, the United States has the highest number of deaths compared to other North American countries.

Greenland, located in the world's Arctic Circle, was the region with the least number of deaths, with only 4,371 deaths

Causes of death

From the graph on the left, we can see that Cardiovascular Diseases are the most common cause of death, followed by Neoplasms and Chronic Respiratory diseases.

On top of that, Lower Respiratory infections and Digestive Diseases ranked fourth and fifth, respectively.




From the graph on the right, we can see increasing trend of the four diseases with the highest number of deaths.

Cardiovascular Diseases is the leading cause of death, and it is also on the rise.

Rankings

The graph on the left is the bar chart ranking the 14 countries with the highest number of deaths.

First of all, we can see that China and India are the two countries with the highest number of deaths, far higher than the United States in third place. The total number of deaths in both countries from 2010 to 2019 has exceeded 80 million.

The United States, in third place, had more than 20 million deaths. Russia and Indonesia followed, along with the remaining nine countries, all with fewer than 20 million deaths

Comparison

The blue line in the graph above shows by default the average number of deaths per year for all countries over the decade (around 250,000). The average is trending upward.

You can choose other countries to compare with the average.

If we choose a few countries to compare with the average, most countries as a whole have an upward trend, even if there is a downward trend in a given year. Among these countries, the annual number of deaths in Russia is on a downward trend.
